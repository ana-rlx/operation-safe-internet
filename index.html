A Cyber Training Course for Kids (Grade 6)

Introduction

This activity is designed to introduce students to basic cybersecurity concepts and safe online behavior. Through guided explanations, real-life examples, and interactive exercises, students learn how to identify common online threats such as phishing, weak passwords, and unsafe websites. The activity includes short discussions, practical demonstrations, and a hands-on task where students analyze online scenarios and decide whether they were safe or unsafe. As a final product submitted to the MOOC, students complete a short digital activity and reflect on what they learned about protecting their personal information online.

Objectives

  • To raise awareness of basic cybersecurity concepts and online safety.
  • To help students recognize common digital threats such as phishing emails and insecure passwords.
  • To encourage responsible and safe use of the internet and digital devices.
  • To develop critical thinking skills when evaluating online content and interactions.

Materials & Resources

  • A computer or tablet with internet access.
  • The MOOC platform and public access URL.
  • Presentation slides and visual examples.
  • Digital exercises and short quizzes.

Attention to Diversity

To address different levels within the classroom, the activity included varied tasks with different degrees of difficulty. Basic explanations and visual examples were provided for students with limited digital experience, while optional extension activities were offered to more advanced students. Group discussions and peer support were encouraged to ensure that all students could participate actively and learn at their own pace.

  • Scams can trick people
