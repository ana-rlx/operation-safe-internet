A beginner-friendly MOOC on cybersecurity awareness and safe online behavior
Level: Primary Grade 6

Activity Overview

This activity is designed to introduce students to basic cybersecurity concepts and safe online behavior. Through guided explanations, real-life examples, and interactive exercises, students learn how to identify common online threats such as phishing, weak passwords, and unsafe websites.

The activity includes short discussions, practical demonstrations, and a hands-on task where students analyze online scenarios and decide whether they were safe or unsafe. As a final product submitted to the MOOC, students complete a short digital activity and reflect on what they learned about protecting their personal information online.

Learning Objectives

  • To raise awareness of basic cybersecurity concepts and online safety.
  • To help students recognize common digital threats such as phishing emails and insecure passwords.
  • To encourage responsible and safe use of the internet and digital devices.
  • To develop critical thinking skills when evaluating online content and interactions.

Preparation

Before the activity, the instructor prepared slides, real-world scenarios, and MOOC platform access. Content was adapted for age-appropriateness and coordinated with the class schedule to ensure smooth delivery.

Development

Using a public URL, students explored interactive tasks and reflections. The session combined guided introductions with independent digital exploration and group discussions.

Tools and Resources Used

Computer / Tablet Internet Access MOOC Platform Presentation Slides Digital Quizzes Projector

Attention to Diversity

To address different levels within the classroom, the activity included varied tasks with different degrees of difficulty. Basic explanations and visual examples were provided for students with limited digital experience, while optional extension activities were offered to more advanced students. Group discussions and peer support were encouraged to ensure everyone learned at their own pace.
